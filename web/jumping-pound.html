WEB DESIGN + BRANDING
Jumping Pound Ranch
Client: Karli Paterson
Year: 2021
Role: Web Designer, Graphic Designer, Project Manager
This exciting project involved the creation of a new brand identity that served a wide array of purposes - a wedding and event venue, a glamping site, and a biodiverse polypay sheep and livestock farm. I created the branding and logo, and then made a WordPress website to promote my client’s goals and vision.
ABOUT THIS PROJECT

Nestled in the foothills of the Canadian Rockies, the owners of Jumping Pound Ranch wanted to share the property’s gorgeous rolling vistas, beautiful mountain views, and peaceful creek landscapes.

My client needed a new brand identity and multi-functional website to promote their various services and event venues. I collaborated with the client over multiple sessions to understand their goals and mission as well as researched competitors. Then, I got to work on creating a brand identity that could serve their many audiences yet speak to their proud family legacy and connection with nature.

PROJECT GOALS

The client wanted a high-end feel while still keeping the feeling of the legacy and beautiful vistas customers would experience while on the property.

JPR needed a logo and color story that would serve multiple audiences and a website to promote a wide breadth of services such as wedding and event booking, a sustainable polypay sheep and livestock farm, and glamping.

Final Result

I created a brand color palette and logo with multiple variations in views (vertical, horizontal, favicon, social) and colors (color, white, black, reverse). The client also wanted a specialty logo to promote the farm, featuring a Polypay sheep as a focal point.

The website was created with the high-end rural glamping aspect in mind. As my client’s business grew, the website also needed growth potential. To that end, I used WordPress so it would be easy to customize and so that the platform would have the potential for easy expansion in the future.
