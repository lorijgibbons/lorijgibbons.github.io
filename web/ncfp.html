WEB DESIGN
Natural Canadian Fur Products
Client: Karli Paterson
Year: 2020
Role: Web Designer, Graphic Designer
Natural Canadian Fur Products (NCFP) were looking for a new, branded image to re-establish itself as a modern fur company and replace its outdated, 4-year-old look. I had the pleasure of refreshing the NCFP brand and website to help them promote and sell ethically harvested furs.
ABOUT THIS PROJECT

My clients were looking for a new, branded image to replace their outdated, 4-year-old look.

Since their competitive advantage is sourcing material from raw goods sustainably harvested in the wild and their commitment to using local First-Nation sewers who hand make every product. I wanted the logo and product materials to look connected to nature. After researching competitors and understanding the client, a new logo was created to appeal to the audience's love of the Canadian outdoor lifestyle.

I refreshed the website's look with new colors, a new theme, new pages, and social engagement. Take a peek at the style guide I created! Assets such as product tags and business cards were printed on brown bag paper differentiating NCFP from the competitors.

A streamlined website was built to showcase featured products, upcoming trade shows, and pop-up shops. Stylistic elements, such as green call to action buttons, were developed with nature in mind to support NCFPâ€™s dedication to sustainable harvesting practices.

PROJECT GOALS

The client's commitment to sustainable harvesting practices was the galvanizing idea behind this rebrand. They wanted a brand with soul - a brand that conveys a commitment to heritage and nature.

Final Result

As a result, a unique and rustically-polished web presence and product materials were crafted that speak to the past, yet can serve as an identity for years to come. Bespoke product tags promote their handmade furs using sustainably harvested raw materials and first-nation sewers. A responsive website showcases a modernized feel with traditional underpinnings.
